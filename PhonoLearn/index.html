Consonants

Plosives

Sounds made by completely blocking airflow and then releasing it suddenly (e.g., /p/, /b/).
/p/
Pen /pɛn/ Spin /spɪn/ Cup /kʌp/
/b/
Bat /bæt/ Rob /rɒb/ Labor /ˈleɪbər/
/t/
Top /tɒp/ Stop /stɒp/ Cat /kæt/
/d/
Dog /dɒɡ/ Bed /bɛd/ Kid /kɪd/
/k/
Cat /kæt/ Skip /skɪp/ Back /bæk/
/g/
Go /ɡəʊ/ Big /bɪɡ/ Dog /dɒɡ/

Fricatives

Sounds made by forcing air through a narrow gap, creating friction (e.g., /f/, /s/).
/f/
Fan /fæn/ Lift /lɪft/ Rough /rʌf/
/v/
Van /væn/ Love /lʌv/ Move /muːv/
/θ/
Thin /θɪn/ Path /pɑːθ/ Birth /bɜːθ/
/ð/
This /ðɪs/ Brother /ˈbrʌðər/ With /wɪð/
/s/
Sun /sʌn/ Face /feɪs/ Kiss /kɪs/
/z/
Zoo /zuː/ Buzz /bʌz/ Easy /ˈiːzi/
/ʃ/
She /ʃiː/ Wash /wɒʃ/ Nation /ˈneɪʃən/
/ʒ/
Measure /ˈmɛʒər/ Vision /ˈvɪʒən/ Garage /ˈɡærɑːʒ/
/h/
Hat /hæt/ Behind /bɪˈhaɪnd/ Who /huː/

Affricates

A combination of a plosive and a fricative, starting with a block and ending with a release through a narrow gap (e.g., /tʃ/, /dʒ/).
/tʃ/
Chair /tʃɛər/ Watch /wɒtʃ/ Future /ˈfjuːtʃər/
/dʒ/
Jump /dʒʌmp/ Badge /bædʒ/ Soldier /ˈsəʊldʒər/

Nasals

Sounds where airflow is blocked in the mouth but released through the nose (e.g., /m/, /n/).
/m/
Man /mæn/ Drum /drʌm/ Come /kʌm/
/n/
No /nəʊ/ Sun /sʌn/ Run /rʌn/
/ŋ/
Sing /sɪŋ/ Thing /θɪŋ/ Long /lɒŋ/

Approximants

Sounds where articulators come close but don't create significant friction; airflow is relatively free (e.g., /l/, /r/).
/l/
Leg /lɛɡ/ Bell /bɛl/ Family /ˈfæməli/
/r/
Red /rɛd/ Carry /ˈkæri/ Street /striːt/
/w/
Wet /wɛt/ Away /əˈweɪ/ Queen /kwiːn/
/j/
Yes /jɛs/ Onion /ˈʌnjən/ You /juː/

/ɒ/

/ʊ/

/ə/
About /əˈbaʊt/ Teacher /ˈtiːtʃər/ Comma /ˈkɒmə/

Vowel sounds produced with a longer duration (e.g., /iː/, /ɑː/).

/uː/

Diphthongs

Diphthongs

/aʊ/

/ɪə/

/eə/

/ʊə/
